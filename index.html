Small overview of the global wealth distribution.
What is the total amout of global wealth ?
The definition of wealth, or “net worth,” as proposed by the researchers at Crédit Suisse is to sum all financial assets and substract any debts.

The methodology can be questioned, in particular in regards to the value of the geography of intangible assets or cultural sites.
How is this wealth shared between countries?
And now, how is this wealth shared between regions ?
First, we can see that Latin America and Africa are far behind the other continents. Taken together, they only control 3.89% of the world's wealth.
In the same way, the U.S., Europe and Asia control comparable amounts of the world's wealth, indicating how important trade relashionships are to the global economy.

Finally, we can see that Asia Pacific have a higher net worth than the U.S. !
How spread out are the region's wealth ?
We can see here that in Asia, North America, Europe and Latin America, a small number of outsiders overpass the rest of their countries. On the other hand, Africa doesn't seem to have any outsider.

Much of this can be explained by the astonishing growth in fortunes of the richest people. For example, the world’s 500 wealthiest people saw their fortunes grow by an incredible $1.2B last year alone, bringing their net worth to $5.9B. To put that in perspective, that’s more than the total wealth for all of Africa ($4.11B).

If this continues, the difference between regions will continue to grow exponentially.
In conclusion, we see that the U.S. still is in the lead on wealth control. The total net worth of Americans is significantly higher than the rest of anybody else in the world.

However, Asian countries collectively possess a net worth larg is essence bigger than the U.S. .

Finally, our little journey illustrate how the U.S., Europe and Asia now control similar amounts of wealth. The world is wealthier today due to globalisation for the most part.
